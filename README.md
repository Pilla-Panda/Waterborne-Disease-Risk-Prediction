# Waterborne-Disease-Risk-Prediction
A machine learning model to predict the risk of waterborne diseases based on water quality parameters and environmental factors 


💧 Waterborne Disease Prediction
📌 About the Project
This project was developed as part of Smart India Hackathon (SIH), aimed at solving a real-world public health problem in rural India.
The primary goal is to predict the risk of waterborne diseases in rural areas using Machine Learning, helping the government and health authorities take proactive measures before outbreaks occur.

🎯 Problem Statement
Rural communities in India often lack access to clean water, making them highly vulnerable to waterborne diseases like Cholera, Typhoid, Dysentery, and Diarrhea. 
Early prediction and detection of disease risk can save lives and reduce the burden on the healthcare system.

🚀 What We Built

A Machine Learning model trained on water quality and environmental data
Predicts the likelihood of waterborne disease outbreaks in a given area
Designed to assist government bodies and rural health workers in early intervention

🛠️ Tech Stack

Python
Scikit-learn / TensorFlow 
Pandas, NumPy
Matplotlib / Seaborn

🤝 Impact
This model is a first step toward building a complete early warning system for waterborne diseases, empowering the government to protect rural communities with data-driven decisions.
